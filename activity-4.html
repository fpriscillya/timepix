Work out the half-life, step by step

Write each answer yourself.

StepYour answer
1. Net rate = KCl rate minus background rate
2. Corrected activity = net rate multiplied by 5
3. Mass of K-40 in your sample (KCl is 52% potassium, natural potassium is 0.012% K-40)
4. Number of K-40 atoms (divide mass by 40 g/mol, multiply by 6.022 times 10 to the 23)
5. Half-life estimate using Activity = 0.693 divided by half-life, multiplied by number of atoms
6. How does your estimate compare with the accepted value of 1.25 billion years?
